Duties and responsibilities

  • Give cadets basics and certify troopers when prompted. (Read how to properly certify people down below)
  • Preferably host trainings.
  • During trainings if there are a lot of people you are to help “keep the peace” and enforce red comms when need be.
  • A “senior NCO” is defined as 1SGT, but evaluation is required before being allowed senior NCO tasks, including; changing roles and names of other people. If you play around with these permissions (such as changing somebody’s name as a joke), then you are likely going to see your permissions removed.
  • Make sure troopers remain in armour while in training, in pre-lobby and main lobby instances.
  • As NCO, you can discipline as you see fit, within reason. When a trooper or fellow NCO (let's hope not) misbehaves by breaking rules or cheating, you are allowed to, and should, deal with it. Ways to do so can be to take them to the side and talk, warn them, hold off their points for a period of time, lock them at their current rank or take away points entirely. Demoting is a CO duty, so if you believe that is in order you can request the COs to do so. These punishments can scale in severity/frequency/duration according to the severity/frequency of their behavior. If their behavior is really out of line and disrupting training, you have the right to kick them from training. Whenever you take any of these action, you are to write a report of what happened and what you did in #reports.

Senior NCO Duties:

So being a senior NCO is simple, basically just help give roles in the requests channel. That being said, there are a few things you must check prior to giving a role.

  • 1) First you must look at the time they request it, if it's right after training and there is no certification log then just give it time, do not grant certifications without the log. Check back the next morning for the log, if none then ask the trooper who trained them.
  • 2) When a trooper requests a rank up (especially if they are new) be sure to check the credit tracker channel to make sure points are being subtracted on rank up and added properly. (Also make sure it's the right amount of points for the rank, check the rank channel). You also need to check attendance logs, search by user ID mentions in the attendance channel.
  • 3) When giving roles make sure they have all the proper roles for their MOS, auxiliary, etc. They should also have the category roles and 87th Harbor Legion role if they are in this server. Only give the category roles if they have some role in it ofc.
  • 4) Along with role changing you must also change their name rank. Call signs should also be similar to their VRC unless they are NCO status.
  • 5) When a trooper asks to have an MOS or auxillary that will replace their old, mention they will lose their other and ask for confirmation.
  • 6) For specialized MOS' tell them to contact the lead to join, then request the role again when approved. (An MOS or special auxiliary that has a lead)
  • 7) We have trainee roles for a reason, use them.

Certification guide:

  • Step 1: Give cert to enlisted (only if you're able to/are lead for that MOS).
  • Step 2: Chart that you gave a cert to the trooper in nco certifications.
  • Step 3: Tell them to request the role in the request channel. (They will not get their role unless it’s been charted in the certification channel)
  • Recruit one person monthly, failure will result in a demotion and points set to zero. (Terms and conditions apply. Read the pinned message in NCO chat.)

Rules with recruiting

  • The age limit to join is 13+, but if they show maturity then an exception can be made. (State if their age is an exception in the reports channel, multiple NCOs+ will have to verify if they are mature during their first couple trainings) That being said, do not allow an immature person to join just because they are over the age limit.
  • If you are giving basics and one of them is being quite obnoxious or disruptive then drop that cadet, do not continue taking them into the server.
  • Ask if they have Discord, heavily preferred; also ask if they have been in any other PvP groups, if so, ask the name of those groups and their server size.

How to give basics

This should take around twenty or so minutes!!

Take them to Mygeeto or a citadel (for example)

FIRST IMPRESSIONS ARE EVERYTHING! Discipline the new troopers, but be more friendly and gentle with the new troops. “Teach them, do not discipline them; there is a difference.” - Zen I think

As a new NCO, the first two basics you give will be supervised by a senion NCO or CO.

Things to go over in basics:

  • Coms:

    • - Red: Remain silent or mute your mic. Don't make them repeat it.
    • - Yellow: Speak freely, but keep it down.
    • - Green: Speak freely.

  • Formations:

    • - Alpha: Columns of 5 next to each other, starting from the front on a line.
    • - Bravo: Shoulder to shoulder on a line or against a wall, STS for short.

  • Faces:

    • - Front Face: Face the direction of those who called.
    • - Left Face: Turn 90° left.
    • - Right Face: Turn 90° right.
    • - Center Face: Turn towards who called it.
    • - About face: Turn 180°.

    Unless left face is called, turn over your right shoulder.

  • Weapon Systems (Naming may differ from other servers):

    • - 2.0: Old, deprecated system, may still be used if people really want to. Latency heavy (aim where your target is headed), most often no visible health bar, and guns respawn on release. Guns deal splash damage and self damage, choke up on your weapon or risk hitting yourself.
    • - 3.0: More stable system, still works fine but not often used. Little to no latency to hit (aim on target), but hits may register on delay, most often has visible health bar, and guns float for a couple seconds on release before respawning. Guns don't deal self damage (explosives excluded).
    • - 3.5: Current system, more stable that 3.0 and very similar. Depending on the instance/version of the system, health bar may be visible to all, only you, only others, or not at all. Guns have physics and drop on release before respawning on a couple seconds delay. You can holster weapons in your body in this system.

    Give examples and, if necessary, take them to an instance of that weapon system.

  • Credits:

    Otherwise called points, are used to rank up. You get 200 points per training attended, present and active over half its runtime, and reacted green on the training announcement. You get 100 per recruit, though if they are not in the Discord server then you must provide your VRC name so NCO's can track it for you. As an enlisted (which you are), you need 600 points for your next rank. Points stack, so if you choose not to request a promotion yet, forget to do so or it hasn't been granted yet, you just keep adding up. When your promotion is granted, subtract the points you need from the ones you have, keeping the remaining points for the next rank up.

  • MOS:

    Once you have 600 points and rank up as previously mentioned, you’ll become a trooper. As a trooper you can go down almost any of the MOS paths in the channels in the bottom of the Discord server. You can also grab an auxiliary such as medic, pilot, airborne, etc. on the side; Just make sure to check if has a rank requirement or not.

  • Teammate Flagging:

    Real simple but not everyone does it, try not to flag your teammates. What this means is don’t point your gun at teammates in battle. Point only at what you mean to shoot

  • Name tags must be off during combat:

    This is an important rule, please make sure your name tags are turned off during combat. Do this under settings -> nameplates -> nameplate visibility -> hidden.

    Also, we ask you to put your status on orange during trainings, so people don't randomly join. If someone requests to join you, ask the host of the training first.

  • Peaking:

    When peaking around a corner, you must uncover at least 50% of your body when you're shooting. So no shooting around corners with just your gun.

  • B-hopping:

    Aka bunny hopping, wait 2 seconds before jumping again. Try not to jump while in combat unless necessary.

  • Dual Wielding:

    • - Pistols: Only for ARC troopers and CO's, unless specified otherwise for a game mode. Can be held as secondary for any MOS, but must be holstered while using your primary.
    • - Bacta Gun: Can be held along with a pistol or carbine. Can be held as secondary for any MOS with medic aux, but must be holstered while using your primary (excluding pistol and carbine). Can't be used as secondary when using a shield.

  • During this cadet training, you're encouraged to have a sparring match using any weapon you find interesting (including dual wielding, server rules apply) so you can find out what path you wish to follow. Not all weapons may be present on some maps.

    Make sure they read the server rules and announcements, as these may include combat rules not stated here.

  • If you already know what path you want to follow before/after some sparring, great! If not, stick to the cadet loadout of pistol and/or carbine during trainings (unless told otherwise for a game mode) and spar outside of training if you want to use the other guns.

  • There are 3 paths to follow in our server: Assault, heavy and scout. Depending on the path, you can advance into different advanced and elite MOS, some having an exclusive auxiliary.

  • Any NCO can train you in basic MOS, but for advanced and elite MOS you must contact its lead to request training. If you have any questions, feel free to ask any NCO, CO or secretary; either on discord or on VRC. If you have a general question for anyone to answer, we have a questions channel.

Finally, pick a CT number and a callsign, so people can tell who you are. Your CT number consists of a 4 digit number and your nickname must be similar to your VRC name (the same, a part of it or easily deduced) so we can tell who you are between discord and VRC. Put this in the request channel or simply ask whoever is training you so it can be changed in the server.

Weapon Rules

Weapons without cooldown restrictions in any combat system:

  • Carbine

  • Pistol

  • Advanced Marksman

Heavy:

  • Light Heavy

    2.0: Can burst fire max of 3 seconds, cooldown for however long you hold it down.

    3.0: Max 6 on half off. (Round up the seconds).

    3.5: No cooldown.

  • ADV Heavy:

    2.0: Can burst fire for max of 5 seconds, cooldown for however long you hold it down.

    3.0: Max 6 on half off. (Round up the seconds).

    3.5: No cooldown.

Marksman:

  • Light Marksman:

    2.0/3.5: No restrictions.

    3.0: 1 shot per second.

Explosives:

  • Grenade Launcher:

    Any system: 3 seconds after smoke disappears.

  • Rocket Launcher:

    2.0: 6 seconds after the smoke disappears.

    3.0: 10 seconds after the smoke disappears.

    3.5: Build in cooldown.

Medgun:

  • 2.0 Combat: Wait the cooldown of your target after the sound finishes.

    • Only allowed to self heal when not in combat targeting you primarily, and you are in cover.
    • Self target is 5 seconds after the sound.

    To chain heal Yourself -> Ally -> Ally -> Yourself you would heal yourself, wait three seconds after the sound then heal the ally, repeat again, then wait 5 seconds after the sound to heal yourself.

    3.0/3.5 Combat: No cooldown, no self healing whatsoever.

Other Cooldown Rules:

  • Shotgun:

    1 second between shots.

  • Chain Gun:

    2.0: Banned

    3.0: max 6, half off

  • Dual Pistol:

    2.0/3.0: 240 BPM alternating shots.

    3.5: No cooldown.

  • Flame Thrower:

    2.0: 3 on 3 off.

    3.0: Banned.

    3.5: No cooldown.

  • Spec Ops Rifle (Silenced/Burst):

    No restrictions

  • Westar:

    Ask Zenobu or Austin.

  • Melee:

    Don’t use it through a shield, a carbine or pistol is allowed with it.

  • Shield:

    Carbine only, fully uncover yourself when firing in that direction. Grenades are allowed.

Auxiliary Rules:

  • Pilot (Trooper rank+):

    You can fly any airship. You can shoot out of the cockpit, though when you are flying you may only have a pistol. You must park and stop completely before firing. To be trained as a pilot, contact Privateiudex.

  • Airborne (Private rank+):

    You can jump out of aircrafts and high places unlike other troopers. You can air glide. To be trained as airborne, contact Eclipse.

Invis Rules:

Some MOS/avatars have the ability to go invisible. Here are the rules on that:

  • 10 seconds to shoot once out of invis
  • 15 seconds to capture a point once out of invis
  • 30 seconds after last shot to go invis
  • Do not shoot while invis

These rules are not yet set in stone and may change
